Why won’t my garage door open or close?

If your garage door won’t open or close, it’s more than just an inconvenience—it can be a sign of underlying issues that need immediate attention. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Munroe Falls Ohio we specialize in diagnosing and resolving these common garage door problems efficiently and effectively.
Power and Remote Issues
The first thing to check is whether the garage door opener has power. Ensure that the opener is plugged in and that the circuit breaker hasn’t tripped. If the opener is functional but the door still won’t move, the issue might be with the remote control. Try replacing the batteries or reprogramming the remote to see if that resolves the issue.
Obstructions and Sensors
A common cause for a garage door that won’t open or close is obstructions or malfunctioning sensors. Check for any debris blocking the door’s path or sensors. The safety sensors located near the floor must be aligned properly and free of dirt. Misaligned or dirty sensors can prevent the door from functioning correctly.
Broken Springs or Cables
Garage door springs and cables bear the weight of the door, making them critical to its operation. If the springs or cables are broken, the door will be unable to open or close properly. Look for signs of wear, such as gaps in the springs or frayed cables. Replacing these components typically requires professional assistance for safety.
Track and Roller Issues
Inspect the tracks and rollers for any obstructions or damage. A door that’s off track or has damaged rollers may not operate smoothly. Professional repair services can realign the tracks and replace any damaged rollers to restore proper function.

How should I replace or repair broken springs safely?

Dealing with broken garage door springs can be daunting, but ensuring their safe repair or replacement is crucial for the smooth operation of your garage door. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Munroe Falls Ohio we provide expert guidance on handling broken springs effectively. Here’s how you can approach the situation safely.
1. Understand the Risks
Garage door springs are under significant tension and can pose serious risks if not handled properly. Attempting DIY repairs without the right tools and knowledge can lead to injury or further damage. It’s often best to consult a professional for safe and reliable repairs.
2. Identify the Type of Spring
Garage doors typically use torsion springs or extension springs. Torsion springs are mounted above the door and operate by twisting, while extension springs are located along the sides and stretch to lift the door. Understanding the type of spring you have helps in sourcing the correct replacement and repair techniques.
3. Secure the Area
Before replacing or repairing springs, ensure the garage door is fully closed and secured. This prevents movement during the repair process and reduces the risk of accidents. Use safety supports to keep the door in place.
4. Replace Springs with Caution
If replacing springs, choose high-quality, durable springs that match the specifications of your garage door. Installing new springs involves careful adjustment of tension to ensure proper function and balance. It’s crucial to follow manufacturer guidelines or seek professional help to avoid improper installation.
5. Regular Maintenance
To prevent future issues, schedule regular maintenance for your garage door. Lubricate springs and check for signs of wear and tear to extend their lifespan and ensure safe operation.
