Why won’t my garage door open or close?

If your garage door won’t open or close, it can be both frustrating and inconvenient. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Westvale New York we understand how vital a functioning garage door is for your daily routine. Here’s why your garage door might be experiencing these issues and what you can do about it.
1. Power Supply Issues
The first thing to check is whether your garage door opener is receiving power. Ensure the opener is plugged in and the circuit breaker hasn’t tripped. Sometimes, a simple power reset can solve the problem.
2. Remote or Wall Switch Problems
If your garage door opener is unresponsive, the issue might be with the remote or wall switch. Check if the remote batteries need replacement or if the wall switch is functioning correctly. Reprogramming the remote or fixing the wall switch might be necessary.
3. Faulty Garage Door Springs
Garage door springs are essential for the proper functioning of your door. Broken or worn-out springs can prevent the door from opening or closing. If you hear a loud bang or notice the door is uneven, your springs might be the problem and should be replaced by a professional.
4. Obstructions and Misalignment
Sometimes, objects blocking the door’s path or misalignment can cause it to malfunction. Inspect the tracks for any obstructions and ensure the door is properly aligned with the tracks. Lubricating the tracks and rollers can also help.
5. Sensor Issues
Modern garage doors are equipped with safety sensors. If these sensors are misaligned or obstructed, the door may not operate correctly. Clean and realign the sensors to ensure they function properly.

How should I replace or repair broken springs safely?

Dealing with broken garage door springs can be a challenging and potentially hazardous task. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Westvale New York we emphasize the importance of safety and precision when it comes to replacing or repairing these crucial components. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to handle broken garage door springs safely.
1. Assess the Situation
Before proceeding, carefully inspect the broken spring. Garage door springs come in two types: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are mounted above the door, while extension springs are located along the sides. Identifying the type of spring you have will guide your repair approach.
2. Disconnect the Opener
For safety, disconnect the garage door opener from the power source. This will prevent accidental activation while you’re working on the door.
3. Do Not Attempt DIY Repairs
Garage door springs are under extreme tension and require specialized tools and knowledge to replace. Attempting a DIY repair can lead to serious injury or further damage. Instead, contact a professional garage door technician who is trained in handling and replacing springs safely.
4. Professional Replacement
A professional will first ensure the door is properly supported before removing the broken spring. They will then carefully install a new spring, making sure it’s correctly calibrated to maintain balanced door operation. Proper installation is crucial for the door’s safety and functionality.
5. Regular Maintenance
To prevent future issues, schedule regular maintenance with your garage door technician. This will ensure that your springs and other components are in good condition and functioning properly.
