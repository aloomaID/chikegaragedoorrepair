Why won’t my garage door open or close?

If your garage door won’t open or close, it’s more than just an inconvenience—it can be a sign of underlying issues that need immediate attention. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Langston Oklahoma we specialize in diagnosing and resolving these common garage door problems efficiently and effectively.
Power and Remote Issues
The first thing to check is whether the garage door opener has power. Ensure that the opener is plugged in and that the circuit breaker hasn’t tripped. If the opener is functional but the door still won’t move, the issue might be with the remote control. Try replacing the batteries or reprogramming the remote to see if that resolves the issue.
Obstructions and Sensors
A common cause for a garage door that won’t open or close is obstructions or malfunctioning sensors. Check for any debris blocking the door’s path or sensors. The safety sensors located near the floor must be aligned properly and free of dirt. Misaligned or dirty sensors can prevent the door from functioning correctly.
Broken Springs or Cables
Garage door springs and cables bear the weight of the door, making them critical to its operation. If the springs or cables are broken, the door will be unable to open or close properly. Look for signs of wear, such as gaps in the springs or frayed cables. Replacing these components typically requires professional assistance for safety.
Track and Roller Issues
Inspect the tracks and rollers for any obstructions or damage. A door that’s off track or has damaged rollers may not operate smoothly. Professional repair services can realign the tracks and replace any damaged rollers to restore proper function.

How should I replace or repair broken springs safely?

Dealing with broken garage door springs can be a challenging and potentially hazardous task. At Chike Garage Door Repair, serving Langston Oklahoma we emphasize the importance of safety and precision when it comes to replacing or repairing these crucial components. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to handle broken garage door springs safely.
1. Assess the Situation
Before proceeding, carefully inspect the broken spring. Garage door springs come in two types: torsion springs and extension springs. Torsion springs are mounted above the door, while extension springs are located along the sides. Identifying the type of spring you have will guide your repair approach.
2. Disconnect the Opener
For safety, disconnect the garage door opener from the power source. This will prevent accidental activation while you’re working on the door.
3. Do Not Attempt DIY Repairs
Garage door springs are under extreme tension and require specialized tools and knowledge to replace. Attempting a DIY repair can lead to serious injury or further damage. Instead, contact a professional garage door technician who is trained in handling and replacing springs safely.
4. Professional Replacement
A professional will first ensure the door is properly supported before removing the broken spring. They will then carefully install a new spring, making sure it’s correctly calibrated to maintain balanced door operation. Proper installation is crucial for the door’s safety and functionality.
5. Regular Maintenance
To prevent future issues, schedule regular maintenance with your garage door technician. This will ensure that your springs and other components are in good condition and functioning properly.
